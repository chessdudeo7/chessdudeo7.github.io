Overview

The Ottawa Math Team Contest (OTMaC) is a regional math competition for high school students in the Ottawa area. As one of the organizers, I built and maintain the official website to serve as the primary public face of the contest.

The site needed to be clean, accessible, and informative — giving students, parents, and teachers a single place to learn about the contest, find preparation materials, and register their teams.

Features

📋

Contest Information

Clear breakdown of contest format, rules, eligibility, scoring, and important dates for the current year.

📚

Preparation Resources

Links to past papers, recommended resources, and topic guides to help students prepare effectively.

📝

Registration

Simple registration flow for team sign-ups, with confirmation and contact information for organizers.

📣

Announcements

News section for updates on contest logistics, results, and future events.

How It Was Built

1

Requirements Gathering

Worked with the OTMaC organizing team to identify what information the site needed to communicate and what the target audience — mostly high school students and their teachers — would expect.

2

Design & Layout

Designed a clean, readable layout with a clear visual hierarchy. Prioritized mobile responsiveness since many students would access the site on phones.

3

Content Structure

Organized all contest content into logical sections — About, Format, Registration, Resources, and Contact — and wrote the copy in a tone appropriate for the audience.

4

GitHub Pages Deployment

Deployed the site under the OTMaC GitHub organization using GitHub Pages, making it easy for other organizers to contribute updates via pull requests.

Challenges

Designing for a Broad Audience

The site needed to clearly communicate to students, parents, and teachers simultaneously — different audiences with different needs — without feeling cluttered or confusing.

Maintainability

Since multiple organizers might update the site over time, the code needed to be clean, well-commented, and easy to edit even for people who aren't experienced web developers.

What I Learned

This project sharpened my ability to design for a real, non-technical audience rather than just for myself. It also gave me experience with collaborative open-source workflows via GitHub — writing clean, readable code and managing a repository that others contribute to. It's rewarding to see a site I built being actively used by students across Ottawa every year.
